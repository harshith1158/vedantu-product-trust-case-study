# **1\. Executive Summary**

## **Objective**

This document analyzes Vedantu's current product ecosystem from a Product Management perspective to identify opportunities for improving users' privacy experience while maintaining a seamless learning journey.

Rather than treating the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 as merely a compliance requirement, this strategy explores how privacy can become a competitive product advantage by strengthening user trust through thoughtful product design.

The purpose of this strategy is not to redesign Vedantu's existing privacy framework, but to identify experience gaps where users lack contextual understanding of how their educational data is collected, processed, and used.

This document serves as the foundation for the accompanying Product Thinking Deck and Clickable Prototype.

---

## **Problem Context**

Vedantu already demonstrates strong investment in:

* Privacy Policy  
* Child Safety Policy  
* Parent Consent  
* Secure Infrastructure  
* Live Classroom Moderation  
* Learning Analytics  
* Personalized Education  
* AI-powered Learning Experiences

From publicly available research, it is evident that Vedantu has already established a mature privacy and security ecosystem.

Therefore, this case study does **not** assume that Vedantu is non-compliant with DPDP.

Instead, it asks a different question:

**How might Vedantu make its existing privacy commitments more understandable, transparent, and trustworthy without increasing user friction?**

---

## **Core Insight**

Privacy today largely exists as legal documentation and background infrastructure.

Users rarely experience privacy as part of their learning journey.

Students often grant permissions without understanding why they are needed.

Parents receive academic visibility but relatively little contextual visibility into how their child's educational data supports personalized learning.

Teachers contribute significant instructional content but have limited product-facing visibility into how recordings, analytics, and profile data are managed.

This creates an **Experience Gap** rather than a **Compliance Gap**.

---

## **Product Opportunity**

Instead of introducing additional consent screens or legal disclosures, Vedantu can evolve its privacy experience using three design principles:

* Smart Consent  
* Just-in-Time Disclosure  
* Progressive Transparency

These principles ensure users receive the right information at the right moment while preserving a frictionless learning experience.

---

## **Proposed Product Vision**

Project TRUST proposes an experience layer integrated into Vedantu's existing workflows that helps students, parents, and teachers understand:

* What data is being collected  
* Why it is required  
* How it improves learning  
* What controls they have

The objective is not to ask users for more permissions.

The objective is to answer users' questions before they need to ask them.

---

## **Expected Outcomes**

For Users

* Increased trust  
* Greater transparency  
* Better understanding of AI-powered learning  
* Easier privacy controls

For Business

* Higher parent confidence  
* Reduced privacy-related support requests  
* Stronger adoption of personalization features  
* Better long-term retention

For Vedantu

* Stronger DPDP alignment  
* Differentiated trust experience

## **DPDP Principles Mapped to Vedantu**

| DPDP Principle | Current Vedantu State | Product Observation | Opportunity |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Purpose Limitation | Strong | Purpose often explained in policies rather than at the point of interaction | Contextual explanations during key actions |
| Consent | Present | Primarily obtained during account creation or feature access | Progressive, context-aware consent |
| Data Minimization | Largely followed | Educational data collection is necessary for personalization | Clearly communicate why each important data type is collected |

| Children's Data Protection | Strong | Parent consent and child safety policies exist | Enhance transparency for parents rather than introducing more consent |
| Security | Strong | Security measures are robust but largely invisible | Reinforce confidence through product communication rather than technical details |

---

## **Key Observation**

Vedantu already aligns well with the intent of DPDP.

The remaining opportunity lies in transforming **policy transparency into product transparency**.

Our proposal will **not** redesign legal compliance.

---

### **Option C**

Redesign the Privacy Policy

**Rejected**

Reason:

---

### **Option D**

Introduce Progressive Transparency Throughout Existing User Journeys

**Selected**

Reason:

This approach increases trust without increasing onboarding friction.

It enhances existing interactions rather than redesigning them.

The opportunity is to make privacy visible, contextual, and understandable without slowing the learning journey.**

# **Version 1 Summary**

## **Observe**

I audited Vedantu's existing product ecosystem, stakeholder journeys, privacy architecture, and user experience.

## **Understand**

I identified that Vedantu's challenge is not regulatory compliance but an **experience gap** where users often lack contextual understanding of how their educational data supports learning.

## **Prioritize**

After evaluating multiple opportunities, I deliberately rejected solutions that increase friction—such as additional onboarding steps or privacy tutorials—and selected **Progressive Transparency powered by Smart Consent and Just-in-Time Disclosure** as the most balanced direction.
