# **1\. PRODUCT OBJECTIVE**

## **Objective**

Define the single measurable outcome that determines whether Project TRUST is successful.

Rather than measuring legal compliance, we focus on product outcomes.

---

## **Product Objective**

**Increase User Confidence without Increasing Learning Friction.**

This objective reflects the central philosophy of Project TRUST.

The product should help users better understand how their educational data supports learning while preserving the fast, uninterrupted experience that students expect from Vedantu.

---

## **Success Definition**

A successful solution should:

* Increase transparency.  
* Increase confidence.  
* Preserve onboarding speed.  
* Maintain personalization quality.  
* Avoid unnecessary consent fatigue.

---

## **Product Success Statement**

Users should feel more informed without feeling interrupted.

---

# **14\. FINAL REFLECTION**

## **Product Summary**

Project TRUST is not a privacy redesign.

It is a trust redesign.

Rather than asking users to trust Vedantu through policies, the platform earns trust through thoughtful, contextual interactions integrated into the learning experience.

By combining Smart Consent, Just-in-Time Disclosure, and Progressive Transparency, the proposed solution aligns with the intent of the DPDP Act while preserving the speed, simplicity, and personalization that define Vedantu's product.

# **PROJECT CONCLUSION**

### **Observe**

I audited Vedantu's existing ecosystem instead of assuming deficiencies.

### **Understand**

I identified an **experience gap**, not a compliance gap.

### **Prioritize**

I deliberately rejected solutions that increased friction and selected a lightweight Trust Layer integrated into existing journeys.

### **Design**

I designed Smart Consent, contextual explanations, and learning-data transparency that answer users' questions without interrupting learning.

### **Measure**

I defined success through measurable product outcomes, rollout strategy, diagnostic thinking, and operational metrics.
